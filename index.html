Model details

Air density is computed from temperature and altitude:

rho = p / (R * T) p = 101325 * exp(-altitude / 8500) T = tempC + 273.15

Wind is treated as a relative airspeed vector:

V_rel_x = speed + windSpeed * cos(windAngle) V_rel_y = windSpeed * sin(windAngle) V_rel = sqrt(V_rel_x^2 + V_rel_y^2)

Drag force uses the effective airspeed:

F_drag = 0.5 * rho * CdA * V_rel^2

Rolling resistance is proportional to weight. The effective rolling resistance coefficient depends on both the tire choice and the road surface. We compute an effective coefficient as:

Crr_effective = Crr_tire * road_factor Road Factor Multiplier: road_factor (Pristine Asphalt) = 0.85 road_factor (Good Asphalt) = 1.00 road_factor (Aged Asphalt) = 1.15 road_factor (Coarse Chip-Seal) = 1.40 road_factor (Broken Road/Cobbles) = 1.80

With the effective coefficient the rolling force becomes:

F_rr = Crr_effective * mass * g = (Crr_tire * road_factor) * mass * g

Gravity force uses the gradient as a slope fraction:

F_gravity = mass * g * gradient

Total power is the sum of forces times speed, adjusted for drivetrain efficiency:

P = (F_drag + F_rr + F_gravity) * speed / (efficiency / 100)

This calculator solves for speed given power, mass, CdA, wind, gradient, tire resistance, and road surface.
